The AI Trust & Execution Framework

The GRC Cycle: Cascading Risk into Cyber, DevOps, AIOps, and Data
  • The Paradigm Shift: AI security is not an IT project; it is the output of a continuous Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) cycle.
  • The Problem: Companies write policies for DPA/NIST compliance, but fail to translate them into engineering. The LLM becomes an exfiltration risk.
  • The Objective: A unified architecture where Risk translates Governance into hard mandates for Data, DevOps, Cyber, and AIOps via a deterministic boundary.
"Governance writes the rules, Compliance audits the rules, but Risk is the engine that cascades those rules into technical reality."

The Risk Cascade (The Operational Waterfall)

Satisfying the Three Lines of Defense (3LoD)
  • 1. Data Foundation (The Source): Data Engineering, Classification Tiering, Labeling, and Security.
  • 2. DevOps (The Infrastructure): Policy-as-Code and securing the Execution Boundary.
  • 3. Cybersecurity (The Perimeter): Data Loss Prevention (DLP), SIEM, and Agentic Access Control.
  • 4. AIOps (The Brain): Routing, Latency Optimization, and Drift Monitoring.

The Data Foundation

Data Engineering & Security Prerequisites
  • Classification Tiering & Labeling: Enforce strict tagging protocols (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted/PII) before AI ingestion.
  • Data Protection Act (DPA): Localized warehouses ensuring sovereign control over consumer data.
  • Data Security (RBAC): FIPS-validated encryption and Vector DBs with native access controls to restrict LLM retrieval contexts.
"If our data isn't tiered and labeled at the warehouse level, our AI has no way of knowing if it's violating the DPA."

Cybersecurity & Data Loss Prevention

The Intersection of Data Security & AI Agents
  • AI-Native DLP: Traditional DLP fails inside prompts. We integrate DLP directly into the AI workflow to mask Restricted/PII data before the foundation model.
  • Non-Human Identity Management: AI Agents are high-risk service accounts. We enforce Zero-Trust APIs preventing autonomous traversal of data lakes.
  • SIEM Integration: Blocked DLP events and unauthorized agent actions route directly to the SOC for triage.

DevOps & The AI Execution Boundary

Where Paper Policies Become Hard-Coded Reality
  • Policy-Validation Intercept (PVI): A deterministic middleware "airlock" deployed exactly at the Execution Boundary.
  • Context-Aware Enforcement: If an AI attempts an API call, the PVI reads the payload's Data Labels. Tier 1 exfiltration attempts are severed mathematically.
  • Immutable Infrastructure: Boundary rules are deployed as Policy-as-Code via CI/CD, preventing tampering by AI or unauthorized users.

AIOps & Human Empowerment

Operating Safely Without Destroying Latency
  • Dynamic Routing: Low-risk queries (Tier 3 Data) bypass heavy scrutiny via regex "Fast-Pass", maintaining sub-100ms latency.
  • Decision Materiality Engine (DME): High-risk actions halt autonomous execution and route to a Subject Matter Expert (SME) Review Dashboard.
  • Explainable AI (XAI): When blocked, users receive plain-text rationales (e.g., "Blocked: DPA violation via Tier 1 Data"), satisfying EU AI Act transparency.

Observe (Audit & 3rd Line of Defense)

Feeding the Compliance Loop Back to GRC
  • OSCAL-Ready Audit Receipts: Continuous generation of verifiable JSON receipts proving SP 800-53 controls and DLP are functioning.
  • Statistical Drift Triggers: AIOps monitors Population Stability Index (PSI). High drift alerts the Risk department to reassess the model.
  • Independent Verification: Internal Audit (3rd Line) mathematically verifies compliance without interrupting 1st Line operations.
"Because our operational teams share one Risk framework, Internal Audit can verify our compliance at any given second."

The "Golden Thread" of Traceability

Visualizing an Auditor's Request

"How do you prevent an AI from leaking PII protected by the DPA?"

  • 5. Compliance: The JSON Audit Log proves the Agent was stopped.
